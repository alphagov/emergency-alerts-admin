Security

GOV.UK Emergency Alerts is built for the needs of government services. It has processes in place to:

  • protect user data
  • keep systems secure
  • manage risks around information

Data

On Emergency Alerts, data is encrypted when:

  • it passes through the service
  • it’s stored on the service

The Cabinet Office acts as data processor for Emergency Alerts. Your organisation is the data controller.

Data Protection Act

Emergency Alerts complies with data protection law. To make sure it stays compliant, there are regular legal reviews of the service’s:

  • privacy policy
  • terms of use
  • approach to data sharing

Technical security

Other technical security controls on Emergency Alerts include:

  • compliance with National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) Cloud Security Principles
  • protective monitoring to record activity, and raise alerts about any suspicious activity
  • using JSON Web Tokens, to avoid sending API keys when your service talks to Emergency Alerts

User permissions and signing in

You can set different user permissions in Emergency Alerts. This lets you control who in your team has access to certain parts of the service.

Two-factor authentication

To sign in to Emergency Alerts, you’ll need to enter:

  • your email address and password
  • a text message code that Emergency Alerts sends to your phone

If signing in with a text message is a problem for your team, contact us to find out about using an email link instead.

Information risk management

Our approach to information risk management follows NCSC guidance. It assesses:

  • how Emergency Alerts is built
  • the infrastructure Emergency Alerts is built upon
  • support for the Emergency Alerts service

This approach also applies to the Notify service that the Emergency Alerts service uses to send emails and SMS messages for account set-up and two-factor authentication.

How we manage risks on Emergency Alerts

Things we do to manage risks on Emergency Alerts include:

  • formal risk assessments based on ISO 27005:2011 and National Cyber Security Centre guidance
  • CHECK-based testing, both annually and when any major changes are made to Emergency Alerts
  • residual risk statement preparation and active management of the risk treatment plan
  • regular updates to the Privacy Impact Assessment
  • security impact assessments

Cabinet Office approval

Emergency Alerts has been assessed and approved by the Cabinet Office Senior Information Risk Officer (SIRO). The SIRO checks this approval once a year.

Emergency Alerts also has approval from the Office of the Government’s SIRO to host data within the EEA.
