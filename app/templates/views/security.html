Security

GOV.UK Emergency Alerts has processes in place to:

  • protect user data
  • keep systems secure
  • manage risks around information

Data

On GOV.UK Emergency Alerts, data is encrypted when:

  • it passes through the service
  • it’s stored on the service

The Cabinet Office acts as the data processor for GOV.UK Emergency Alerts. Your organisation is the data controller.

Data Protection Act

GOV.UK Emergency Alerts complies with data protection law. To make sure it stays compliant, there are regular legal reviews of the service’s:

  • privacy policy
  • terms of use
  • approach to data sharing

Technical security

Other technical security controls on GOV.UK Emergency Alerts include:

  • compliance with National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) Cloud Security Principles
  • protective monitoring to record activity, and raise alerts about any suspicious activity

Sensitive information

You should not store sensitive or personal information on GOV.UK Emergency Alerts, including when you’re working with templates or in training mode.

User permissions and signing in

You can set different user permissions in GOV.UK Emergency Alerts. This lets you control who in your team has access to certain parts of the service.

Two-factor authentication

Enter your email address and password to sign in to GOV.UK Emergency Alerts.

You’ll also need either:

  • a text message code that GOV.UK Emergency Alerts sends to your phone
  • a YubiKey connected to your account

If your team cannot sign in with a text message, contact the GOV.UK Alerts team to find out about using an email link instead.

Information risk management

Our approach to information risk management follows NCSC guidance. It assesses:

  • how GOV.UK Emergency Alerts is built
  • the infrastructure GOV.UK Emergency Alerts is built upon
  • support for the GOV.UK Emergency Alerts service

This approach also applies to Notify and its service providers when sending two-factor authentication text messages.

How we manage risks on GOV.UK Emergency Alerts

The things we do to manage risks on GOV.UK Emergency Alerts include:

  • formal risk assessments based on ISO 27005:2011 and NCSC guidance
  • CHECK-based testing, both annually and when any major changes are made to GOV.UK Emergency Alerts
  • residual risk statement preparation and active management of the risk treatment plan
  • regular updates to the Privacy Impact Assessment
  • security impact assessments

Cabinet Office approval

GOV.UK Emergency Alerts has been assessed and approved by the Cabinet Office Senior Information Risk Officer (SIRO). The SIRO checks this approval once a year.

Classifications and security vetting

You can use GOV.UK Emergency Alerts to send alerts at ‘OFFICIAL’ classification.

The GOV.UK Emergency Alerts platform should not store any information graded at ‘OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE’ or higher. All information should be cleared for release to the public under the Government Security Classifications policy.

GOV.UK Emergency Alerts does not process data classified as ‘SECRET’ or ‘TOP SECRET’.

The GOV.UK Emergency Alerts team has Security Check (SC) level clearance from United Kingdom Security Vetting (UKSV).
