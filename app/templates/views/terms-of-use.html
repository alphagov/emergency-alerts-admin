Terms of use

These terms apply to your use of GOV.UK Emergency Alerts.

When using Emergency Alerts

You must:

  • complete your organisation’s information assurance process (you do not need to include the Emergency Alerts team or our delivery partners, we’ve already done that)
  • tell us immediately if you have any security breaches
  • keep your API keys secure, if you have them
  • ensure you are given and maintain the right levels of consent for access to the system to author and manage alerts
  • check that the data you add to the Emergency Alerts system is accurate and complies with data protection legislation
  • not author, manage or attempt to send unauthorised emergency alerts
  • only send alerts that meet the thresholds set out in the Emergency Alert protocol

Emergency Alerts will:

  • send alerts, as long as they meet our guidelines
  • keep your data secure
  • give you one month’s notice by email if we change our terms of use

Unauthorised release of alerts

Sending unauthorised emergency alerts is a criminal offence and you could be prosecuted and sent to prison.

Contact the Crisis Management team within the COBR unit to get the latest version of the Emergency Alert protocol, which sets out guidelines for sending alerts.

Crisis Management team (COBR)
emergencyalerts@cabinetoffice.gov.uk

Leaving Emergency Alerts

You can contact the Emergency Alerts team at any time to remove your access.

Read our privacy notice to find out how long we keep your personal data.
